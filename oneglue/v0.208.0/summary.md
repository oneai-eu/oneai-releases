OneGlue v0.208.0 adds console access to an SCVMM VM via its Hyper-V host: a new "Console (via Hyper-V)" action opens the VM's console through the Hyper-V host (relayed by the on-site OneGlue agent), so you can reach VMs on isolated customer networks that a direct remote-desktop connection can never touch — even before the OS boots. Secure attention (Ctrl-Alt-Del) is one click away, and the applicable domain/shared credential can be revealed for you to type at the login (console access authenticates to the host; the guest login stays in your hands), every reveal audit-logged. Also: when connecting directly by RDP you can now choose the security mode (NLA default, or TLS) for machines whose RDP server refuses the default negotiation. Governed by design — console reuses the org-grantable "connect to VM" permission, cross-org/cross-tenant access impossible, every console session and credential reveal recorded in the audit log (metadata only).
